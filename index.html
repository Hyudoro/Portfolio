Gnu/Linux

GNU/Linux is a free and open-source operating system that combines the Linux kernel with the tools and libraries from the GNU project. Initially developed by Linus Torvalds in 1991 as a kernel for an operating system, Linux was combined with the GNU system, initiated by Richard Stallman in 1983, to create a complete operating system. GNU/Linux is renowned for its robustness, security, and flexibility, making it a popular choice for servers, workstations, and embedded systems. Its open-source nature means that anyone can use, modify, and redistribute it, contributing to its rapid adoption and evolution through the contributions of thousands of developers worldwide. GNU/Linux offers a wide variety of distributions (distros), such as Ubuntu, Fedora, and Debian, each with its own sets of software and package managers, allowing users to customize their experience based on their specific needs. In addition to its flexibility, GNU/Linux is known for its compatibility with standards and powerful networking capabilities, making it ideal for software development, system administration, scientific computing, and personal use. Its active community and abundant resources provide extensive support and learning opportunities for both new users and experienced professionals.

C-language

The C language plays a crucial role in the GNU/Linux project due to its simplicity, performance, and control. As a system programming language, C is used to develop many essential components of the operating system, including the Linux kernel itself. Its ability to directly access hardware and manipulate low-level structures makes it a preferred choice for operating system development like Linux. Through C, developers can write efficient and optimized code to control hardware resources, manage memory, and interact with the Linux kernel. Additionally, C is widely supported by development tools and libraries used in the GNU/Linux project, facilitating code integration and maintenance. In summary, the C language is essential in the GNU/Linux project as it enables the design and construction of the operating system's foundations, ensuring its stability, performance, and compatibility with a wide range of hardware.

BTS-SIO

(Slam)


The BTS SIO (Information Services to Organizations) with the SLAM (Software Solutions and Business Applications) option is a two-year post-baccalaureate program designed to prepare students for careers in software development and software project management. This option focuses on learning the technical and theoretical skills necessary for designing, developing, and maintaining software solutions tailored to the needs of businesses. Students explore various programming languages, databases, as well as project management methodologies. The goal is to train specialists capable of effectively addressing current IT challenges by developing web applications, mobile applications, or custom software, while considering the technical and economic constraints of organizations.

SISR


The BTS SIO (Information Services to Organizations) with the SISR (Infrastructure Solutions, Systems, and Networks) option is a two-year post-baccalaureate program designed to prepare students for careers in network and computer systems administration. This option focuses on learning the technical and theoretical skills necessary for implementing, securing, and maintaining business IT infrastructures. Students explore various operating systems, networking technologies, and virtualization solutions, while learning to manage IT services and equipment. The goal is to train specialists capable of effectively addressing current IT challenges by ensuring the availability, security, and performance of organizational information systems.
